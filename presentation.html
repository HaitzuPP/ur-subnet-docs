The thesis

A real-revenue subnet: emissions are anchored to actual usage, not vanity metrics

Real revenue

Operators run a paid VPN/privacy service. Their on-chain deposits are funded by genuine customer income — not token printing.

Structural α sink

To compete for emissions, operators must buy and lock the subnet’s α every block. That is recurring demand most subnets lack.

Underwritable

Because deposits are bounded by revenue, total deposits (ΣDT) become an on-chain fundamental you can diligence.

The asterisk: until VERIFIER §10 ships, the quality signal behind emissions is provisional.

Risks

Four risks to underwrite before the thesis pays

High

Smart-contract custody

The contract holds all deposits and captured emission. Audit quality and the “finalized claims are sacrosanct” rule are load-bearing.

High

Verification is provisional

v1 proves transit, not honest relay. Emission “quality” is monitoring-grade until VERIFIER §10 lands.

Medium

Governance concentration

Launch is owner-multisig + upgradeable proxy — the pattern behind 2026’s centralisation backlash. Timelock must ship at mainnet.

Medium

Pooling & off-chain competition

Aggregating workers under one UID has precedent (ComputeHorde, IOTA/SN9), but on-chain Merkle payout to 100k+ providers is unproven — and per-miner Yuma competition moves off-chain to the verifier, so it’s only as robust as §10.
